# Nirman CRM

register: product

## Product purpose
Dead-simple CRM for small Indian real-estate builders. Reps work leads
(status → follow-up → call), the builder head sees everything, outsiders
(partner brokers, reception) get exactly one slice each. Sold per-project
monthly prepaid, collected in UPI/cash by the founder.

## Users
- Front-line sales rep: often 40+, Hindi-first, mid-range Android, works
  outdoors in bright daylight between phone calls. Zero training budget —
  the standing bar is "can a 45-year-old rep use it correctly, first try,
  no training?" (the 45-year-old-rep test).
- Builder head/admin: owner-operator, checks numbers on phone + web.
- Team leader / partner agency / receptionist: narrow, role-scoped slices.

## Brand & tone
Evergreen (deep green #132A21 family) + brass (#A8823C/#C9A354) on warm
ivory (#F6F3EC). Calm, trustworthy, unhurried. Hindi-first microcopy where
it matters (billing warnings). Tone: a helpful senior colleague, never a
gamified app.

## Anti-references
- Espo/Odoo and every "enterprise CRM": the USP is simplicity against them.
- Gamified sales apps (confetti, streaks, leaderboard noise).
- 90s-desktop look: boxy bordered panels, serif display headings (the
  original Fraunces headers read dated — retired 2026-07-12).

## Strategic principles
1. The rep core loop (status → follow-up → call) is load-bearing; never
   behind a gate or a redesign whim.
2. Absence over greyed-out; capability- and usage-gated UI (progressive
   disclosure specs, 2026-07-12).
3. Big touch targets, high contrast, daylight-legible. Density is for the
   head's dashboards, not the rep's list.
4. Familiar patterns over invention. The tool disappears into the task.
